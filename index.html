Customer Classification: Featured/Not Featured

In this panel, we can identify Featured and Not Featured customers in a selected period. In this case, for a customer to be considered featured, we compare the sales versus the previous year and based on their percentage variation, we determine if they are Featured or Not Featured.
And here comes the most interesting part:

  • There is a parameterized selector where we define the growth % that customers must meet, the range is between 0% and 100%
  • Includes a Slicer to display only FEATURED or NOT FEATURED customers
  • There is a scatter plot that helps us determine the relationship between Sales and % Variation versus the previous year
This allows us to access valuable information from multiple sources. We can segment by Executive, Year, and much more that you may want to visualize.

RFM Analysis

This is a Business Intelligence technique based on three key aspects:

  • Recency (when was the last interaction)
  • Frequency (how often does the interaction occur)
  • Monetization (how much does the customer spend)

By assigning scores or categories to these factors, customers can be segmented into specific groups to develop personalized marketing strategies. This analysis helps identify the most valuable customers, increase retention and loyalty, and improve the company's profitability.

Vehicle Report - BORCELLE Rental Car

This is a real business case from a Car Rental company that needed a Dashboard to visualize key performance indicators (KPIs).

It's important to highlight that the data for this report is sourced from multiple CSV files hosted in a Google OneDrive account. These files are updated weekly through an automated process managed by Power BI Service.

The purpose of this "Dashboard" is to provide the car rental company with a quick and effective view of its performance in these key aspects of the business. Visualizing these KPIs can help the company make informed and strategic decisions to improve efficiency, safety, and customer satisfaction, while also reducing risks and losses.
